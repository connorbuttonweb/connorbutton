HYROX Race — Toronto

World Series of Fitness Racing • Enercare Centre, Exhibition Place • Solo division

Lead-up. Through the summer I was putting in long days as a landscaping foreman and then training after shift — late-night runs, zone-2 volume, and station practice wedged between early mornings. The goal was consistency: build an engine, learn the stations, and show up prepared even when the legs were cooked. That discipline was the difference maker heading into race day.

Race. HYROX is eight 1 km runs broken up by eight stations. I kept the opening pace relaxed and focused on tidy transitions. Here are my station times from Toronto:

SkiErg4:51
Sled Push2:39
Sled Pull4:54
Burpee Broad Jumps6:27
Row5:03
Farmer’s Carry2:33
Sandbag Lunges6:08
Wall Balls7:52

Full analytics + lap breakdown: HYRESULT. Official finish time: 1:35:14. (Source: HYRESULT race page.)

Takeaways. Pacing the runs kept me steady for the heavy stations, and clean Roxzone entries/exits saved free seconds. The big gains for next time: faster cadence on the sled pull, shorter rests on burpees, and more wall-ball density late. I’m rolling this into the next block with two HYROX-style sessions per week, a long aerobic run, and strength work aimed at grip and posterior chain — plus pencilling in a couple of local races to keep the engine honest.
